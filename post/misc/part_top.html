my petpet

Ezzie is my little soreen, gifted to me by my owner as a welcome home present when I was first adopted, and I just adore him. He doesn't do so well outside of water (soreens skins dry up very quickly!) so a lot of the time I have to leave him with a sitter or friend whenever I'm away. I'm lucky he's so easy to look after, except that he can get pretty demanding when he wants to play!

I did try the keep-him-in-a-jar thing briefly so that I could bring him along, but I ended up paranoid that I'd misplace him somewhere. Plus, I think it's kinda cruel when he's such a high-energy creature

I often get asked if I'm going to paint him, seems its such a common thing to do these days. I do kinda like the green and electric colours on soreens, but also kinda hope some new colours will be discovered, so I probably won't be painting him anytime soon.

In addition, here are some tips that may be helpful for your organization! First of all, say, there is an image with an original height less than 575px, you might want to put line breaks -- (br)s -- before the second image tag, and after it. This may help position the image nicer for you. This is demonstrated in the last code segment, of the Food Faerie PNG. However, when using line breaks, remember not to go overboard! Once the height is over 600px, anything that comes next won't display. Secondly, if you don't want a caption/title, you can set max-width to 600px instead of the default 575. This allows a plant-full display of the image.

Lawd I went through some chonky phases
It's so funny looking back, I almost want to try recreating something in that style that was so hot and trendy back then.
Let's do it, for the heck of it.

How to make 2000s dodge&burn art!

Step 1. We want some fully sick lineart, so let's bang out some thumbnails to work out what direction to go in. (Now this I did not do as a kid. Big mistake lol.) One important thing we can't forget is some vast background environment (with probably too many details in it), so I'm going to go with a .......here

Step 2. Lineart styles that are in include thin ballpoint lines or a thin whispy mechanical pencil look
I am going to be drawing them digitally though and not suffer using a fineliner pen like I did as a kid. Thank goodness for having steadier hands now as an adult.
Important Note! Need to make the canvas way too big, like at least 6000px wide so that it scales down horribly... oh wait no, my computer won't even allow it without crashing. Heh, they don't make 'em like they used to. Alright we'll go with 2000px wide, that's still waaaay bigger than I'd ever use now. We didn't appreciate JPG artifacting like we do now.

Step 3. Flat colours onto everything, aw yeah! No subsurface scattering or bounce illumination to consider here, no sirree!
And of course we need a palette that is all the rage, so let's make me a richer, colder purple than my current look

Step 4. Shading time! Using these key tools ICONS HERE. Bit embarrassing that I couldn't even find them at first. Oh no it's already looking bad, idk if we can save it.

Do you know how exciting it was finding these at the age of 12? I was trying every button and dropdown option in Photoshop (Elements 2 lol), trying out everything I could find. And then wow, what's this? An easy shading and highlight button?

Remember how all VHS covers for cartoon movies were shaded like this? What good times we lived in.

I had a school textbook with all the illustrations dodged and burned in the most horrendous way I've ever ever seen. I wish I had it still or could remember what it was called. But the highlights were like this (DEMONSTRATE GIF)

Step 5. Only solution is to keep. Adding. More. Shadows.
Thank you hands. Steady hands are better than a steady relationship, remember that.

Really exaggerate the form, make him scrumptious. Pfft.

Step 6. Extra little highlighty bits now
Squiggly lines where the water caustics are falling
GIF Here
Ooh I love it when they criss-cross over and you get a little bright spot, that's so exciting.

We're gonna want to spend at least 10 hrs on this, so no rush, just take it niiiiice and easy. We've got nowhere to be.

(cut to) Gee this is kinda theraputic actually, maybe I should make slow art more often.

Step 7. I almost forgot, probably should've started with the eye as its the most important thing of all. Make sure I spend a few hours on it to get it as glossy and jewellike as possible.
There, perfect. It's basically rescued the entire picture from disaster.

Step 8. And of course how could I forget, gotta colour that lineart!

Step 9. Let's get into that environment, it must have clouds, and stars, and fireflies for good measure
